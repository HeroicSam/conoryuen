Hotswaps

Following the outbreak of COVID and transition to work from home culture, luxury mechanical keyboards have turned into a rapidly growing industry with a bustling aftermarket. The goal of Hotswaps is to support this growth by bridging the gap between a now huge community and the archaic technology supporting it.

Problem

The keyboard secondary market is made up of a handful of online forums and social media platforms designed for discussion rather than exchange in mind, causing glaring usability problems. The most popular of these is Reddit's /r/mechmarket, shown below.

User Empathy

I conducted user interviews to better understand the fundamental issues affecting keyboard buyers and sellers. We decided this would best give give our team a holistic view of the aftermarket landscape. We recruited and interviewed four /r/mechmarket super users to gauge their behaviors and pain points.

Solution

We solved the problem using a design and feature-oriented approach. Through the interviews and further market research, we found that there were two main contributors to negative user experience: inconvenient user flows and lack of design structure.

User Stories & Design Research

Before wireframing and mockups, I needed to first understand who our target audience was. We put together a survey, which had 95 respondents, distributed to several popular mechanical keyboard forums to gauge our potential users' demographics.

Visual Research

Studying consumer to consumer sites serving other markets allowed us to find industry standard ecommerce designs. This was a critical step because it allowed our team to uniquely tailor Hotswaps' design while also maintaining best practices.
