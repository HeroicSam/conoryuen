Hotswaps

Hotswaps is a curated marketplace helping people buy and sell luxury mechanical keyboards, by providing an efficient, dynamic browsing experience, cultivating community trust and assuring transactions.

This was a personal project I co-founded with a group of friends, with whom we spent countless hours on outside of our day jobs and during the weekends to build this vision.

Background

Following the outbreak of COVID, the mechanical keyboard community experienced massive growth as people began working from home full-time and upgrading their home office setups seeking comfort and ergonomics.

As the first founding member and sole product designer on the team which consisted of 4 engineers and myself, I led business, product, UI, and UX strategy.

Problem Discovery

As a keyboard hobbyist, I was frustrated by the usability issues of prominent consumer-to-consumer keyboard marketplaces. To validate these problems, I recruited 9 users, both buyers and sellers, from the most popular keyboard marketplace (a subforum of Reddit which has 265k subscriberes as of writing this) to interview about the pain points they experienced.

We found five frustrations in particular that were common between multiple participants.

  • Pictureless item catalogs. Many users feel that text-based item catalogs are inconvenient, causing window shopping to be unenjoyable and cumbersome. There are at minimum 2 clicks from the catalog to see what a seller’s product looks like when there could be none.

  • Dissimilar product page layouts. Due to the product listing creation process being mostly open-ended, many item pages do not share information structures. This leads to a lot of time users can spend scanning and scrolling to find the details being sought if it’s even included at all.

Reframing the Problem

Through the interviews and further market research, we found that there were two main contributors to negative user experience: lack of design structure and inconvenient user flows.

Solution

For our minimium viable product, we focused on solving these issues through design and feature-oriented approaches.

User Demographics Research

Before wireframing and mockups, I needed to first understand who our target audience was. We put together a survey, which had 95 respondents, distributed to several popular mechanical keyboard forums to gauge our potential users' demographics.

Visual Research

Studying consumer to consumer sites serving other markets allowed us to find industry standard ecommerce designs. This was a critical step because it allowed our team to uniquely tailor Hotswaps' design while also maintaining best practices.
