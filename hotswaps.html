Hotswaps

Overview

Hotswaps is a curated marketplace helping people buy and sell luxury mechanical keyboards, by providing an efficient, dynamic browsing experience, cultivating community trust and assuring transactions.

This was a personal project I co-founded with a group of friends, with whom we spent countless hours on outside of our day jobs and during the weekends to build this vision.

Role

Co-founder, Product Designer

Product Strategy, User Research, Interaction & Interface Design, Prototyping, User Testing, Product Management

April 2021 - July 2022

Background

Following the outbreak of COVID, the mechanical keyboard community experienced massive growth as people began working from home full-time and upgrading their home office setups for comfort and ergonomics.

As the first founding member and sole product designer on the team which consisted of 4 engineers and myself, I led business, product, UI, and UX strategy.

Key Achievements

  • Transform an idea into a product. Taking part in building something from the very beginning is extremely rewarding and exciting. The experience I had working on Hotswaps is something I hold very close to my heart.

  • Juggled many different hats. For the first eight months, it wasn’t uncommon for me to be developing wireframes, mockups, and prototypes in Figma for four to five hours a day. But with many other roles in running a startup needing filling, I dedicated many other hours to managing our product roadmap, hosting weekly scrum meetings, and creating marketing material.

Problem Discovery

As a keyboard hobbyist, I was frustrated by the usability issues of prominent consumer-to-consumer keyboard marketplaces. To validate these problems, I recruited 9 users, both buyers and sellers, from the most popular keyboard marketplace to interview about the pain points they experienced.

We found five frustrations in particular that were common among multiple participants.

  • Pictureless item catalogs. Many users feel that text-based item catalogs are inconvenient, causing window shopping to be unenjoyable and cumbersome. This leads to unnecessary clicks, making the user flow inefficient and frustrating.

  • Dissimilar product page layouts. Due to the product listing creation process being mostly open-ended, many item pages do not share information structures even within the same item categories. Users become frustrated scanning and scrolling for relevant product information.

  • Biased product visibility. What the item catalog shows a user upon arrival is based on Reddit’s algorithm which was originally designed for discussion and content threads. As a result, the site does not keep an ongoing inventory of available items, making it hard to determine what has and has not been sold.

  • Lack of a formal transaction processor. Although there are recommendations on how users should conduct transactions, they are not rules meant to be enforced. This reduces the safety confidence of newer users and opens the door for scam and fraud.

  • Diffculty gauging user trust. Some users who were new to the platform felt that figuring out a buyer or seller’s reputability was a time-consuming, often fruitless process. Other users who were okay with the process felt that it could be further streamlined.

Product Vision

As a product, our vision is to be the one-stop destination for buying, selling, and exploring mechanical keyboards with 3 main focus areas.

  • Accessiblility. We want to be the gateway to a world where people can type on any keyboard they wish by providing them with the largest selection of mechanical keyboards anywhere.

  • Affordability. We want to help individuals find great deals by providing them with negotiation and price trends tools, ultimately giving people the power of a fair market.

  • Accountability. We want to make buying and selling keyboards as safe and riskless as possible by incentivizing positive behavior and holding users accountable for their past transactions.

Defining the MVP

As a team, we scoped out the high-level features and designs that would encompass our MVP. We further broke these down into smaller user stories and development tickets that formed our roadmap to launch.

Our key features:

  • Personalized product feed. Curate a marketplace catalog of only the brands and items you’re interested in. Whether you know what you’re looking for or just window shopping, you can spend as much or as little time as you’d like finding the right keyboard.

  • User feedback system. Allow users to provide reviews and rate their experience with sellers so that future buyers can have confidence even before they hit the purchase button.

  • Payment processor integration. Stay continuously secure throughout the entire transaction process with the buyer and seller protections afforded to users under PayPal for Marketplaces.

Design

My process for Hotswaps was based in the Double-Diamond design model. I user-tested and gathered feedback at set stages in the product roadmap to ensure that we were constantly iterating on incomplete designs and solving the actual problems users were experiencing.

In devloping the site’s UX, I researched user flows of various marketplaces serving different audiences and industries to understand best e-commerce practices. For our UI, I wanted a design that was clean and minimalistic.

Product catalog page

Our endless product catalog, coupled with a dynamic filtering system, serves as the home and landing page of Hotswaps.

Item listing page

A clean and minimalistic product page layout with features like price comparisons and seller cards to help users make informed purchases.

Checkout flow

Although PayPal for Marketplaces took care of most of the heavy lifting for us, we still needed to handle address selection and order confirmations on our side. We opted for a simple page that allowed users to see their entire order summary and select pre-saved addresses for PayPal's automated checkout workflow.

Listing creation flow

Mechanical keyboards are complex items with a wide variety of types and cateogrizations. I wanted to lower the barriers to entry for sellers of all levels by making it quick and easy to get their items in front of potential buyers.

Messages

Balancing community and commerce is ultimately the core of Hotswaps. By accentuating price information and transparency during user interactions, our hope was that we could limit scalping and over-inflation in the market.

Results and Takeaways

Starting a startup has been one of the most difficult, yet invaluable experiences I've been a part of. Having had a hand in both building a product and managing a team, I learned a lot about planning effectively and knowing the right moments and places to focus my energy.

  • Focus on the MVP. In a small team environment, you can find yourself taking on a lot of unanticipated responsiblity, making time management and prioritization that much more important. Don't get carried away and and focus on the features solving your users' problems.

  • Usability testing is Key. Nothing is more telling than watching real people use your designs and go through your flows. Testing my prototypes with actual users helped cut through my hidden biases, giving me the opportunity to adjust designs before handing them off to devs.

  • Understand Motivation and Morale. Startups are extremely fast-paced and can be overwhelming, leading to burnout and slowed progression. Paying attention to the team's culture and environment allowed me to better understand each team member's personal motivations and best manage each individual's workload.
