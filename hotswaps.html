Hotswaps

Hotswaps is a curated marketplace helping people buy and sell luxury mechanical keyboards, by providing an efficient, dynamic browsing experience, cultivating community trust and assuring transactions.

This was a personal project I co-founded with a group of friends, with whom we spent countless hours on outside of our day jobs and during the weekends to build this vision.

Background

Following the outbreak of COVID, the mechanical keyboard community experienced massive growth as people began working from home full-time and upgrading their home office setups seeking comfort and ergonomics.

As the first founding member and sole product designer on the team which consisted of 4 engineers and myself, I led business, product, UI, and UX strategy.

Problem Discovery

As a keyboard hobbyist, I was frustrated by the usability issues of prominent consumer-to-consumer keyboard marketplaces. To validate these problems, I recruited 9 users, both buyers and sellers, from the most popular keyboard marketplace (a subforum of Reddit which has 265k subscribers as of writing this) to interview about the pain points they experienced.

We found five frustrations in particular that were common among multiple participants.

  • Pictureless item catalogs. Many users feel that text-based item catalogs are inconvenient, causing window shopping to be unenjoyable and cumbersome. This leads to unnecessary clicks, making the user flow inefficient and time-consuming.

  • Dissimilar product page layouts. Due to the product listing creation process being mostly open-ended, many item pages do not share information structures even within the same item categories. Users become frustrated scanning and scrolling for relevant product information.

  • Biased product visibility. What the item catalog shows a user upon arrival is based on Reddit’s algorithm which was originally designed for discussion and content threads. As a result, the site does not keep an ongoing inventory of available items, making it hard to determine what has and has not been sold.

  • Lack of a formal transaction processor. Although there are recommendations on how users should conduct transactions, they are not rules meant to be enforced. This reduces the safety confidence of newer users and opens the door for scam and fraud.

  • Diffculty gauging user trust. Some users who were new to the platform felt that figuring out a buyer or seller’s reputability was a time-consuming, often fruitless process. Other users who were okay with the process felt that it could be further streamlined.

Product Vision

As a product, our vision is to be the one-stop destination for buying, selling, and exploring mechanical keyboards with 3 main focus areas.

  • Accessiblility. We want to be the gateway to a world where people can type on any keyboard they wish by providing them with the largest selection of mechanical keyboards anywhere.

  • Affordability. We want to help individuals find great deals by providing them with negotiation and price trends tools, ultimately giving people the power of a fair market.

  • Accountability. We want to make buying and selling keyboards a safe, riskless activity by cultivating a community of trust.

Defining the MVP

As a team, we scoped out the high-level features and designs that would encompass our MVP. We further broke these down into smaller user stories and development tickets that formed our roadmap to launch.

Our key features:

  • Personalized product feed. Curate a marketplace catalog of only the brands and items you’re interested in. Whether you know what you’re looking for or just window shopping, you can spend as much or as little time as you’d like finding the right keyboard.

  • User feedback system. Allow users to provide reviews and rate their experience with sellers so that future buyers can have confidence even before they hit the purchase button.

  • Payment processor integration. Stay continuously secure throughout the entire transaction process with the buyer and seller protections afforded to users under PayPal for Marketplaces.

Design

My process for Hotswaps was based in the Double-Diamond design model. I user-tested and gathered feedback at set stages in the product roadmap to ensure that we were constantly iterating on incomplete designs and solving the actual problems users were experiencing.

In devloping the site’s UX, I researched user flows of various marketplaces serving different audiences and industries to understand best e-commerce practices.

Because it was important that our brand conveyed trustworthiness to our users, I created a UI design that was clean and minimalistic.
